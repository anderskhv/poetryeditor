## 1. On Love (Gibran)

### Our Analysis Summary
- **Form**: Prose Poetry
- **Key insight**: Love as transformative spiritual force requiring surrender
- **Literary devices identified**: Personification, Paradox (crown/crucify), Extended metaphor (gardener), Anaphora

### Comparison Notes
- PoemAnalysis.com has basic coverage but lacks line-by-line breakdown
- Our line-by-line commentary is a differentiator
- Wikipedia has context but no detailed literary analysis

### Review Checklist
- [ ] Verify poem text matches public domain source (Project Gutenberg)
- [ ] Check line-by-line commentary is accurate to the text
- [ ] Confirm literary devices are correctly identified

## 4. I Live My Life in Widening Circles (Rilke)

### Our Analysis Summary
- **Form**: Free Verse
- **Key insight**: Spiritual seeking as process, not destination
- **Literary devices identified**: Extended metaphor (circles), Tricolon (falcon/storm/song), Temporal expansion

### Comparison Notes
- One of Rilke's most famous poems
- English translations vary - we should note translator
- The falcon/storm/song tricolon is well-recognized

### Review Checklist
- [ ] Verify translation source
- [ ] Check that German title is correct ("Ich lebe mein Leben in wachsenden Ringen")
- [ ] Confirm Book of Hours context is accurate
